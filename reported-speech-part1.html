Key Concept

Key Concept
Direct vs Reported Speech
Direct speech quotes the exact words. Reported speech retells the message — without quotation marks, with tenses and pronouns adjusted.
🎙 Direct Speech — exact words
Quotation marks. Original tense. Original pronouns.
"I saw Eleanor at the bar last night."
— James Webb, hotel porter
"I am completely innocent and my club is legal."
— Rosa Caruso
"that" — optional connector
The word that after the reporting verb is optional. Both forms are correct.
She said that she was innocent. ✅
She said she was innocent. ✅
📋 Reported Speech — your words
No quotes. Tense goes back. Pronouns and time shift.
Webb said (that) he had seen Eleanor at the bar the night before.
Rosa claimed (that) she was completely innocent and that her club was legal.
What changes? — checklist
🕰 Tense — shifts one step back (the backshift)
👤 Pronouns — I → he/she  ·  my → his/her
📅 Time & place — today → that day  ·  here → there
🗣 Reporting verb — said / told / claimed…

said vs told
3 / 8
Step 1
say / said  vs  tell / told
These are the two main reporting verbs. They have one key difference: tell/told always needs a person (object) directly after it. Say/said does not.
SAY / SAID — no object
say / said + (that) + clause
✅ She said she was innocent.
✅ He said (that) he hadn't seen her.
❌ She said me she was innocent.
To mention the listener, use "to":
She said to the detective that she was innocent.
TELL / TOLD — always needs a person
tell / told + person + (that) + clause
✅ Webb told Morrison he was alone.
✅ She told him (that) she knew nothing.
❌ He told that he was innocent.
Common objects: me · him · her · us · them · Morrison · the detective
🔍 Case File — George Cross reports Eleanor's disappearance
George said his wife had disappeared. ✅
George told Morrison his wife had disappeared. ✅
George told that his wife had disappeared. ❌
George said Morrison his wife had disappeared. ❌

The Backshift
4 / 8
Step 2
The Backshift — Tenses Go Back One Step
When we report what someone said, each tense shifts one step into the past. Think of it as rewinding the recording from Room 401.
Direct Speech→Reported Speech
Present Simple  "I work here." → Past Simple  He said he worked there.
Present Continuous  "She is waiting." → Past Continuous  He said she was waiting.
Past Simple  "I saw Eleanor." → Past Perfect  He said he had seen Eleanor.
Present Perfect  "I have found the note." → Past Perfect  She said she had found the note.
will  "I will call you." → would  He said he would call.
can  "I can explain." → could  She said she could explain.
🔍 Webb's full statement — complete backshift
"I saw Eleanor. She is with a man. I will say nothing."
→
Webb said he had seen Eleanor, that she was with a man, and that he would say nothing.

Step 3
Pronouns & Time Expressions Also Change
The backshift isn't enough. Pronouns must reflect the new speaker, and time/place words must reflect the new moment of reporting.
Pronouns change — new speaker's perspective
I / me → he / she · him / her
my → his / her
we / us → they / them
our → their
you → I / he / she / they
Rosa said: "My club is clean."
→ Rosa said her club was clean.
Time & place — new moment of reporting
now → then / at that moment
today → that day
yesterday → the day before
tomorrow → the next day
here → there
this / ago → that / before
George said: "My wife disappeared yesterday from here."
→ He said his wife had disappeared the day before from there.

Reporting Verbs
6 / 8
Step 4
Reporting Verbs — claim, deny, admit, insist
These four verbs let you show how a witness spoke, not just what they said. Each follows a specific grammatical pattern.
CLAIM  + that
Say something is true without proof
"I have never met Eleanor Cross."
↓
Rosa claimed (that) she had never met Eleanor.
DENY  + -ing
Say something is not true — always with -ing
"I never took the money!"
↓
Webb denied taking the money.
ADMIT  + -ing
Confess something you did — always with -ing
"Yes, I lied to the police."
↓
George admitted lying to the police.
INSIST  + that
Say forcefully, repeat with conviction
"I know nothing about the ransom!"
↓
Rosa insisted (that) she knew nothing about the ransom.
⚠️ Pattern
claim / insist + (that) + subject + verb (full clause)    deny / admit + verb-ing — never use "that" + clause with these two

Common Errors

Watch Out!
Common Errors — Part 1
These are the most frequent mistakes. Read the error and try to find the problem before clicking reveal.
Error 1
told without an object
❌ "George told that his wife had disappeared."
✅ George said (that) his wife had disappeared.
✅ George told Morrison (that) his wife had disappeared.
Told always needs an object — a person. Without one, use said.
Error 2
No backshift — tense unchanged
❌ "Webb said the suspect is in Room 401."
✅ Webb said the suspect was in Room 401.
Present Simple → Past Simple. Tenses must shift back when reporting past speech.
Error 3
Pronoun not changed
❌ Eleanor said I was innocent. (reporting Eleanor's words)
✅ Eleanor said she was innocent.
Eleanor's original word "I" must change to "she" — we are now reporting from the outside.
Error 4
deny with "that" instead of -ing
❌ "Rosa denied that she knew Eleanor."
✅ Rosa denied knowing Eleanor.
Deny and admit always take the -ing form, not a "that" clause. Deny knowing / admit lying.
